4. Grant of Rights and Acceptable Use Subject to payment of Subscription Fees, the Provider grants the Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable right to permit Authorised Users to access and use the Services for internal business purposes.

The Customer shall ensure that Authorised Users comply with the Acceptable Use Policy(AUP)in Schedule 1; no unlawful, infringing or harmful content may be uploaded; no attempts to bypass security or perform penetration testing without permission.

The Provider shall provide the Services in accordance with the SLA, with a target uptime of 99.5% per calendar month(excluding Scheduled Maintenance).

Scheduled Maintenance windows will be notified at least 48 hours in advance and occur outside UK business hours where reasonably practicable.

Emergency maintenance may be undertaken to address critical issues; the Provider will use reasonable efforts to notify the Customer promptly.

The Provider shall implement administrative, physical and technical safeguards appropriate to the risk(including access controls, encryption at rest and in transit where appropriate, vulnerability management, logging and monitoring).

The Provider shall maintain an information security program aligned to ISO/IEC 27001(or equivalent)and shall annually test incident response and disaster recovery plans.

The Data Protection Addendum at Schedule 3 applies to any processing of Personal Data by the Provider on behalf of the Customer.

Ensure that only Authorised Users use the Services and keep credentials secure.

Be responsible for the accuracy, quality, legality and integrity of Customer Data.

Not access or use the Services to build a competing product or service.

The Provider shall provide Support Services in accordance with Schedule 4, including response targets by severity.

Subscription Fees are as set out in Schedule 2 and are payable monthly in advance.

Invoices are due within thirty(30)days of the invoice date.

Late payments accrue interest at four percent(4%)per annum above the Bank of England base rate.

Fees exclude VAT, which shall be payable in addition where applicable.

The Provider may suspend access to the Services immediately upon notice if the Customer is in material breach(including overdue fees beyond 30 days)or where suspension is necessary to address a security or legal risk.

The Provider will reinstate access promptly upon remedy.

Neither Party excludes liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, fraud, or any liability which cannot be excluded by law.

Subject to clause 15.1, each Party’s aggregate liability in any twelve(12)month period shall not exceed the total Subscription Fees paid or payable by the Customer in that period.

Neither Party shall be liable for indirect or consequential loss, including loss of profit, revenue, goodwill or data.

Initial Term:two(2)years from the Commencement Date.

Thereafter, automatic one(1)year renewals unless either Party gives not less than thirty(30)days’ notice prior to expiry.

Either Party may terminate for material breach not remedied within thirty(30)days of notice, or upon insolvency of the other Party.

On termination, access ceases.

Exit assistance and data return are addressed in clause 17 and Schedule 3. During the thirty(30)days following termination or expiry, the Customer may export Customer Data via available tools or request a one-off export in a mutually agreed format.

Upon Customer request, the Provider shall delete or return Customer Data and certify deletion, subject to legal retention obligations.

On reasonable notice and during business hours, the Customer may review relevant policies and third-party certifications.

Any on-site audits shall be subject to reasonable security and confidentiality controls.

Neither Party is liable for delay or failure to perform due to events beyond its reasonable control, provided it notifies the other Party and mitigates.

If a Force Majeure event continues for more than sixty(60)days, either Party may terminate on ten(10)Business Days’ notice.
