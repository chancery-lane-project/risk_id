Introduction:California’s Climate Disclosure Laws California is pioneering legally enforceable climate disclosure through:.

CCDAA(Health & Safety Code §38532):Requires companies with over $1B in revenue doing business in California to publicly disclose Scope 1 and 2 emissions annually from 2026, and Scope 3 from 2027. Disclosures must align with the Greenhouse Gas Protocol and be verified by a third party, escalating from limited to reasonable assurance by 2030. 2. CRFRA(§38533):Requires companies with over $500M in revenue to report biennially on climate-related financial risks aligned with the Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures(TCFD), starting January 1, 2026.[a]Reports must describe governance, impacts, risk management, and metrics.

Enforcement:The California Air Resources Board(CARB)[b]will impose penalties up to $500, 000/year(CCDAA)and $50, 000/year(CRFRA)for non-compliance.

Organizations must classify themselves as:* Reporting Entity(CCDAA):Partnership, LLC, corporation, or similar entity with >$1B revenue and operations in California.

* Covered Entity(CRFRA):As above, but with >$500M revenue and not regulated by the California Department of Insurance.

Use this classification to determine contract obligations across subsidiaries, vendors, and partners.

* Ensure calculations use GHG Protocol standards.

The Counterparty shall cooperate fully with third-party verifiers appointed by[the Company][c]to ensure compliance with the Climate Corporate Data Accountability Act(Health & Safety Code §38532), and shall promptly rectify any discrepancies identified during assurance reviews.

Contractual Solutions for Financial Risk Disclosure(CRFRA)Why:Entities must disclose climate-related financial risks and their mitigation strategies aligned with TCFD.

* Require counterparties to disclose material physical and transition risks.

* Establish reporting cadence and content aligned with TCFD.[The Counterparty]shall biennially report climate-related financial risks associated with its operations, including transition and physical risks, in accordance with the TCFD framework.

Reports must be submitted no later than[October 31]of each odd-numbered year.

The Counterparty must disclose governance structures, risk exposure, mitigation actions, and relevant metrics, to support[the Company]'s compliance with the Climate-Related Financial Risk Act(Health & Safety Code §38533).
