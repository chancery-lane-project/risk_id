NEC4 Engineering and Construction Contract

Date: 15 September 2025

1. Parties

Employer: GreenCity Council, a local authority constituted under the laws of England and Wales, of 1 Civic Square, Leeds, LS1 1AB, United Kingdom (“Employer”).

Contractor: BuildRight Infrastructure Ltd, a company incorporated in England and Wales (No. 07345621) of 99 Construction Lane, Manchester, M1 2AA, United Kingdom (“Contractor”).

Together, the “Parties”.

2. Background

  1. The Employer requires the design and construction of the Riverside Bridge Project to improve transport connectivity and public realm.
  2. The Contractor represents that it has the requisite expertise, capacity, personnel and financial standing to execute the Works.
  3. The Parties agree to enter this Contract on the terms and conditions set out below, incorporating the Schedules.

3. Definitions and Interpretation

3.1 Definitions

In this Contract, unless the context otherwise requires:

  • Applicable Law means all laws, statutes, regulations, codes of practice, and guidance applicable to the Works.
  • Compensation Event means an event entitling the Contractor to an adjustment to the Completion Date and/or the Contract Price as described in clause 15 and Schedule 1.
  • Completion means when the Works achieve Practical Completion as certified by the Project Manager.
  • Completion Date means 30 September 2027 or such other date as adjusted in accordance with this Contract.
  • Contract Price means £75,000,000 (seventy-five million pounds sterling), subject to adjustments under this Contract.
  • Defects Period means the period of fifty-two (52) weeks commencing on the date of Completion.
  • Good Industry Practice means the exercise of that degree of skill, diligence, prudence and foresight which would reasonably be expected from a skilled and experienced contractor.
  • Project Manager means the person appointed by the Employer to administer this Contract and certify payments and Completion.
  • Site means the land and other places made available by the Employer for the Works, as described in Schedule 1.
  • Works means all design, construction, testing and commissioning obligations required by this Contract and the Works Information.
  • Works Information has the meaning set out in Schedule 1.

3.2 Interpretation

  • Headings are for convenience only and do not affect interpretation.
  • References to statutes include amendments and re-enactments.
  • “Including” shall be construed as “including without limitation”.

4. Contract Documents and Priority

The Contract comprises: (a) this Contract; (b) the Schedules; (c) any documents expressly incorporated by reference. In the event of conflict, the order of precedence is: this Contract; then the Schedules; then incorporated documents.

5. Scope of Works

  1. The Contractor shall design, construct, test and commission a dual carriageway bridge over the River Aire, together with access roads, drainage, lighting, traffic management, utilities diversions and associated landscaping, all as described in the Works Information (Schedule 1).
  2. The Contractor shall undertake temporary works, traffic and pedestrian management, and protection of the existing environment; maintain public safety and coordinate with stakeholders.
  3. Design responsibility rests with the Contractor, including coordination of design interfaces, obtaining approvals from relevant authorities and compliance with Applicable Law and standards.

6. Employer’s Obligations

  1. Provide access to the Site in accordance with the Site Access Plan in Schedule 1.
  2. Appoint a Project Manager and Supervisor and ensure timely instructions and decisions.
  3. Make payments certified by the Project Manager within the time periods specified in clause 13 and Schedule 3.

7. Contractor’s Obligations

  1. Execute the Works with due skill and care in accordance with Good Industry Practice, the Programme, and the Works Information.
  2. Provide competent personnel, adequate resources, and maintain all necessary insurances per Schedule 4.
  3. Prepare and maintain a detailed Programme (clause 9) and Risk Register (clause 10).
  4. Comply with health and safety requirements, CDM Regulations, and environmental obligations, keeping records available for audit.

8. Programme

  1. Within twenty (20) Business Days of the Contract Date the Contractor shall submit a detailed Programme showing the sequence and timing of the Works, critical path, float, resources, interfaces and key milestones, aligned to Schedule 2.
  2. Programme updates shall be submitted monthly or upon instruction, reflecting progress, changes, and any effects of Compensation Events.
  3. Failure to maintain an up-to-date Programme constitutes a material breach.

9. Early Warning and Risk Register

  1. Each Party shall give early warning of any matter which could increase cost, delay Completion, or impair performance.
  2. The Contractor shall maintain a Risk Register, reviewed monthly, capturing risks, owners, mitigations and residual impacts.
  3. Early warning meetings will be held at least monthly to agree mitigation actions and potential Compensation Events.

10. Quality Assurance and Testing

  1. The Contractor shall operate a quality management system certified to ISO 9001 (or equivalent) and maintain Inspection and Test Plans (ITPs).
  2. Materials and workmanship shall comply with Applicable Law, standards and manufacturer’s recommendations. Certificates of conformity shall be provided upon request.
  3. Testing shall be carried out in accordance with the Works Information. The Employer may witness tests and inspect records.

11. Changes and Compensation Events

  1. Changes to the Works Information, access dates, constraints or other Employer instructions are treated as Compensation Events where they impact time and/or cost.
  2. Valuation of Compensation Events shall be by reference to agreed rates and prices or, where not applicable, on a fair and reasonable basis.
  3. Time and money adjustments will be certified by the Project Manager following assessment.

12. Completion, Defects and Final Account

  1. Completion. When the Works are complete and capable of beneficial use, the Project Manager shall certify Completion and the Defects Period shall commence.
  2. Defects. The Contractor shall rectify Defects notified during the Defects Period within a reasonable time. If the Contractor fails to rectify, the Employer may do so and recover costs.
  3. Final Account. Within sixty (60) Business Days after expiry of the Defects Period, the Contractor shall submit the final account. The Project Manager shall certify the final amount within thirty (30) Business Days thereafter.

13. Payment

  1. The Contractor shall submit monthly applications for payment showing work properly executed, valuation of Compensation Events, variations, retention and amounts previously certified.
  2. The Project Manager shall issue interim certificates within seven (7) Business Days of each application; the Employer shall pay certified amounts within fourteen (14) calendar days of the certificate date.
  3. Retention shall be five percent (5%) of each certified amount, reducing to two-and-a-half percent (2.5%) upon Completion, and released upon expiry of the Defects Period.
  4. Set-off is permitted for amounts due and owing under this Contract upon notice.

14. Performance Security

The Contractor shall provide, within ten (10) Business Days of the Contract Date, a performance bond in the amount of ten percent (10%) of the Contract Price from a surety acceptable to the Employer, remaining in place until Completion.

15. Insurance

Insurance requirements are set out in Schedule 4. Evidence of insurance and premium payment shall be provided prior to mobilisation and upon renewal.

16. Health, Safety and Environment

  1. The Contractor shall be Principal Contractor under the CDM Regulations and discharge all associated duties.
  2. Environmental controls shall include pollution prevention, waste management, noise and dust control, and protection of ecology and watercourses.
  3. All incidents and near misses shall be reported to the Employer within twenty-four (24) hours, with investigation and corrective action plans.

17. Intellectual Property and Confidentiality

  1. Design IP developed specifically for the Works shall vest in the Employer on payment, subject to a non-exclusive licence to the Contractor for the purpose of performing and maintaining the Works.
  2. Each Party shall keep the other’s Confidential Information confidential and use it only for the purposes of this Contract.

18. Force Majeure

A Party is not liable for delay or non-performance caused by events beyond its reasonable control, provided it gives prompt notice and mitigates. If such event persists for more than sixty (60) days, either Party may terminate on ten (10) Business Days’ written notice.

19. Suspension and Termination

  1. Suspension. The Employer may instruct suspension for safety, legal or other reasonable grounds. Time and cost consequences shall be assessed under clause 11.
  2. Termination for Contractor Default. For material breach, persistent failure to proceed regularly and diligently, abandonment, or insolvency, the Employer may terminate on written notice.
  3. Termination for Employer Default. The Contractor may terminate for prolonged non-payment (greater than twenty-eight (28) days after due date) following notice and cure period.
  4. Consequences. On termination, the Project Manager shall certify amounts due for work done, materials on Site, reasonable demobilisation, less amounts previously paid and damages (if applicable).

20. Dispute Resolution

  1. Disputes shall be escalated to senior representatives. If unresolved within twenty (20) Business Days, either Party may refer the dispute to adjudication under the Scheme for Construction Contracts (England and Wales) Regulations.
  2. Either Party may subsequently commence litigation in the courts of England and Wales.

21. General

  1. Assignment/Subcontracting. The Contractor may not assign without Employer consent. Subcontracting specialist trades is permitted provided the Contractor remains liable.
  2. Notices. Notices shall be delivered by hand, recorded delivery or email to the contacts specified in Schedule 1.
  3. Entire Agreement. This Contract and Schedules constitute the entire agreement.
  4. Governing Law and Jurisdiction. England and Wales.

Schedule 1 — Works Information

1. Scope

Design and construction of a dual carriageway steel-composite bridge over the River Aire, including:

  • Abutments, piers, foundations (piles), approach viaducts;
  • Road deck, parapets, expansion joints, waterproofing, surfacing;
  • Drainage, utilities diversions, street lighting, signage and road markings;
  • Landscaping, public realm enhancements and riverbank protection;
  • Temporary works, traffic management and environmental mitigation.

2. Site and Access

Site limits and access routes are shown on drawings WI-001 to WI-010. Working hours: 07:30–18:00 Monday–Friday, 08:00–13:00 Saturday (variations require approval).

3. Standards

Compliance with relevant UK/EU standards including the Design Manual for Roads and Bridges (DMRB), BS EN bridge standards, CDM Regulations, and Employer’s Requirements ER-01 to ER-20.

4. Stakeholders and Consents

Coordination with utilities undertakers, the Environment Agency, navigation authorities and adjacent landowners. Contractor to obtain necessary permits and approvals.
