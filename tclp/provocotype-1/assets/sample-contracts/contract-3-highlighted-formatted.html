1. Parties

Lender: BigBank plc (No. 04321098), 100 Finance Avenue, London, EC2N 3XJ, United Kingdom ("Lender").

Borrower: TechGlobal Holdings Ltd (No. 06123456), 77 Innovation Park, Cambridge, CB4 0GF, United Kingdom ("Borrower").

Together, the "Parties".

2. Definitions and Interpretation

2.1 Definitions

  • Business Day means a day (other than Saturday or Sunday) on which banks are open in London.
  • Commitment means £50,000,000.
  • Drawdown Notice has the meaning in Schedule 1.
  • Final Maturity Date means 31 August 2030.
  • Finance Documents means this Agreement and any other document designated as such.
  • Interest Period means each period selected by the Borrower per clause 6.
  • Loan means each advance made under the Facility.
  • Margin means 2.50% per annum over SONIA.
  • SONIA means the Sterling Overnight Index Average, as published by the Bank of England.

2.2 Interpretation

  • References to a person include bodies corporate, unincorporated associations and partnerships.
  • Headings do not affect interpretation; "including" means "including without limitation".

3. The Facility

  1. The Lender makes available to the Borrower a revolving credit facility in an aggregate amount equal to the Commitment, for general corporate purposes.
  2. Subject to the terms of this Agreement, Loans may be borrowed, repaid and re-borrowed until the Final Maturity Date.

4. Conditions Precedent

  1. Delivery of constitutional documents, board resolutions, specimen signatures and incumbency certificates.
  2. Legal opinions satisfactory to the Lender.
  3. Evidence of authority and compliance with Applicable Law.
  4. As a condition precedent to the first Utilisation, the Borrower shall deliver to the Lender: A completed environmental, social and governance due diligence questionnaire covering the Borrower's governance, greenhouse gas emissions, transition planning, reliance on offsets, and policies on biodiversity and deforestation.The Borrower's latest climate-related disclosures prepared in accordance with the ISSB IFRS S2 standard or, if not available, in line with the TCFD framework.A copy of the Borrower's transition plan, approved by its Board, including interim greenhouse gas reduction targets for 2030 and 2040, together with evidence of independent assurance of reported data. No Loan may be made until the Lender has received and confirmed its satisfaction with the information described above.

5. Utilisation

  1. The Borrower shall deliver a Drawdown Notice by 11:00 a.m. three (3) Business Days prior to the proposed Drawdown Date, specifying the amount (minimum £1,000,000) and Interest Period.
  2. Utilisation is subject to no Event of Default continuing on the proposed Drawdown Date and representations being true and correct.

6. Interest and Interest Periods

  1. Each Loan bears interest at the rate of SONIA plus the Margin.
  2. Interest accrues daily and is payable on the last day of each Interest Period and on repayment.
  3. Interest Periods shall be one, three or six months (as available), selected in the relevant Drawdown Notice.
  4. Default interest: 2% per annum above the rate otherwise applicable to any overdue amount.

7. Fees

  1. Arrangement Fee. 0.50% flat on the Commitment, payable on Signing.
  2. Commitment Fee. 0.25% per annum on the undrawn and uncancelled Commitment, payable quarterly in arrears.

8. Repayment and Prepayment

  1. The Borrower shall repay each Loan in full on the last day of its Interest Period unless such Loan is rolled over in accordance with this Agreement.
  2. All outstanding Loans are repayable in full on the Final Maturity Date.
  3. Voluntary prepayment is permitted with not less than five (5) Business Days' written notice; prepayments shall be accompanied by accrued interest.
  4. Mandatory prepayment upon change of control of the Borrower.
  5. If the Borrower: Fails to deliver the climate report required by clause 11.4 within 120 days of its financial year end; or Is determined by the independent verifier referred to in clause 11.4 to have materially breached its Transition Plan for two consecutive reporting periods;then the Lender may, by written notice to the Borrower, cancel the Commitment and declare all outstanding Loans immediately due and payable.

9. Taxes, Increased Costs and Illegality

  1. All payments shall be made free and clear of withholding taxes unless required by law. If withholding is required, the Borrower shall gross up the payment (subject to customary exceptions).
  2. If the Lender incurs increased costs due to changes in law or regulation, the Borrower shall, upon demand, indemnify the Lender for such additional costs.
  3. If it becomes unlawful for the Lender to maintain the Facility, the Lender may cancel its Commitment and declare the Loans due and payable.

10. Representations and Warranties

  1. The Borrower is duly incorporated and validly existing; has power to own its assets and carry on its business; and has the power and authority to enter into and perform this Agreement.
  2. This Agreement constitutes legal, valid and binding obligations of the Borrower.
  3. No Default is continuing and no litigation is pending or threatened which could have a material adverse effect.

11. Undertakings

11.1 Information Undertakings

  • Deliver audited annual financial statements within 120 days of year-end and quarterly management accounts within 30 days of quarter-end.
  • Promptly notify the Lender of any Default.

11.2 General Undertakings

  • Maintain all authorisations and comply with Applicable Law.
  • Negative pledge: not to create security over its assets (other than permitted security) without Lender consent.

11.3 Financial Covenants

  • Interest Cover Ratio ≥ 3.0:1 (tested quarterly).
  • Net Debt to EBITDA ≤ 3.5:1 (tested quarterly).

11.4 Climate Reporting

The Borrower shall deliver to the Lender, within 120 days of each financial year end:

A report of its Scope 1, Scope 2 and material Scope 3 greenhouse gas emissions for that year, prepared in accordance with the GHG Protocol Corporate Standard.

Details of progress against its climate transition plan (the "Transition Plan"), including interim targets for 2030 and 2040.

A statement of reliance on carbon offsets, specifying volumes, types, and standards used, with confirmation that all offsets meet the Oxford Principles for Net Zero Aligned Carbon Offsetting.

The report shall be accompanied by a certificate signed by a director of the Borrower and independently verified by an assurance provider accredited under ISO 14064-3.

11.5 Sustainability Performance Adjustment

If, in any financial year, the Borrower achieves all of the following KPIs (the "Climate KPIs"):

(a) a year-on-year reduction of at least 7% in combined Scope 1 and Scope 2 greenhouse gas emissions relative to the Baseline Year 2024.

(b) maintenance of a Board-approved Transition Plan aligned with the Paris Agreement goal of limiting global warming to 1.5°C.

(c) And confirmation that no more than 10% of claimed reductions rely on carbon offsets, and that all such offsets comply with the Oxford Principles for Net Zero Aligned Carbon Offsetting.

Then the Margin shall be reduced by 0.05% per annum for the following Interest Period.

If the Borrower fails to deliver the climate report required under clause 11.4, or an independent verifier concludes that the Borrower has materially misstated its Climate KPIs, the Margin shall increase by 0.05% per annum for the following Interest Period.

Any Margin adjustment shall not exceed ±0.10% per annum in aggregate.

12. Events of Default

Each of the following is an Event of Default:

  • Non-payment of any amount due under a Finance Document (subject to a two Business Day grace period for administrative error).
  • Breach of financial covenant or other undertaking not remedied within any applicable cure period.
  • Misrepresentation in any material respect.
  • Insolvency, insolvency proceedings or analogous events.
  • Cross-default: default under Financial Indebtedness exceeding £5,000,000.
  • Unlawfulness or repudiation of a Finance Document.

On and at any time after an Event of Default, the Lender may cancel the Commitment, declare all or part of the Loans immediately due and payable, and/or enforce any rights.

13. Costs and Indemnities

The Borrower shall, within three (3) Business Days of demand, pay the Lender the amount of all reasonable costs and expenses (including legal fees) incurred in connection with the negotiation, preparation, execution, administration and enforcement of the Finance Documents.

14. Confidentiality

The Parties shall keep Confidential Information confidential and use it solely for the purposes of this Agreement, subject to customary exceptions for disclosures required by law or regulators.

15. Assignment and Transfer

The Lender may assign or transfer its rights and obligations to another lender of equivalent standing subject to confidentiality undertakings. The Borrower may not assign without Lender consent.

16. Miscellaneous

  1. Notices. As set out in Schedule 1.
  2. Amendments. Must be in writing and signed by the Parties.
  3. Governing Law and Jurisdiction. England and Wales; courts of England have exclusive jurisdiction.

Schedule 1 — Drawdown Mechanics and Notices

A. Drawdown Mechanics

  1. Borrower delivers a Drawdown Notice (by email and PDF) by 11:00 a.m. three (3) Business Days before the Drawdown Date.
  2. Notice specifies amount (≥ £1,000,000 and in integral multiples of £100,000), Interest Period, and payment instructions.
  3. Conditions Precedent satisfied; no Default continuing; representations true in all material respects.

Schedule 2 — Repayment Schedule

All Loans are repayable in full on the Final Maturity Date unless prepaid. The Borrower may prepay Loans in whole or part with five (5) Business Days' notice; partial prepayments must be ≥ £500,000 and in integral multiples of £100,000.

Schedule 3 — Covenants (Summary)

Financial Covenants

  • Interest Cover ≥ 3.0:1 (EBITDA / Net Interest Expense).
  • Leverage ≤ 3.5:1 (Net Debt / EBITDA).

Information Covenants

  • Annual audited statements within 120 days; quarterly within 30 days.
  • Compliance Certificates signed by CFO each quarter.

General Covenants

  • Negative pledge; restrictions on disposals; permitted acquisitions/indebtedness baskets as agreed with the Lender.
