1. Parties

Provider: CloudFusion Ltd (No. 07223344), 22 Tech Street, London, EC1A 1DD, United Kingdom ("Provider").

Customer: BrightWave Consulting Ltd (No. 06598710), 5 Innovation Way, Bristol, BS1 4TR, United Kingdom ("Customer").

2. Background

  1. The Provider operates a cloud-based project management platform and related services.
  2. The Customer wishes to subscribe to the Services for internal business use on the terms of this Agreement.
  3. The Parties acknowledge the importance of reducing the environmental and climate impacts associated with digital services, and intend to collaborate in good faith to align the Services with recognised sustainability standards and the UK's net zero commitments.

3. Definitions and Interpretation

3.1 Definitions

  • Authorised Users means employees and contractors authorised by the Customer to use the Services.
  • Data Protection Legislation means UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.
  • Personal Data has the meaning given in Data Protection Legislation.
  • SLA means the service levels in Schedule 4.
  • Services means the hosted software and support described in Schedule 1.
  • Subscription Fees means the fees set out in Schedule 2.
  • Term means the Initial Term and any Renewal Terms per clause 16.

3.2 Interpretation

  • Headings do not affect interpretation; "including" means "including without limitation".

4. Grant of Rights and Acceptable Use

4.1 Subject to payment of Subscription Fees, the Provider grants the Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable right to permit Authorised Users to access and use the Services for internal business purposes.

4.2 The Customer shall ensure that Authorised Users comply with the Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) in Schedule 1; no unlawful, infringing or harmful content may be uploaded; no attempts to bypass security or perform penetration testing without permission.

5. Services and Service Levels

5.1 The Provider shall provide the Services in accordance with the SLA, with a target uptime of 99.5% per calendar month (excluding Scheduled Maintenance).

5.2 Scheduled Maintenance windows will be notified at least 48 hours in advance and occur outside UK business hours where reasonably practicable.

5.3 Emergency maintenance may be undertaken to address critical issues; the Provider will use reasonable efforts to notify the Customer promptly.

6. Security

6.1 The Provider shall implement administrative, physical and technical safeguards appropriate to the risk (including access controls, encryption at rest and in transit where appropriate, vulnerability management, logging and monitoring).

6.2 The Provider shall maintain an information security program aligned to ISO/IEC 27001 (or equivalent) and shall annually test incident response and disaster recovery plans.

7. Data Protection

The Data Protection Addendum at Schedule 3 applies to any processing of Personal Data by the Provider on behalf of the Customer.

8. Customer Obligations

  1. Ensure that only Authorised Users use the Services and keep credentials secure.
  2. Be responsible for the accuracy, quality, legality and integrity of Customer Data.
  3. Not access or use the Services to build a competing product or service.

9. Support Services

The Provider shall provide Support Services in accordance with Schedule 4, including response targets by severity.

10. Fees and Payment

10.1 Subscription Fees are as set out in Schedule 2 and are payable monthly in advance. Invoices are due within thirty (30) days of the invoice date.

10.2 Late payments accrue interest at four percent (4%) per annum above the Bank of England base rate.

10.3 Fees exclude VAT, which shall be payable in addition where applicable.

11. Suspension

The Provider may suspend access to the Services immediately upon notice if the Customer is in material breach (including overdue fees beyond 30 days) or where suspension is necessary to address a security or legal risk. The Provider will reinstate access promptly upon remedy.

12. Intellectual Property

12.1 All IPR in the Services and underlying software are owned by the Provider or its licensors.

12.2 The Customer owns all IPR in Customer Data. The Customer grants the Provider a non-exclusive licence to process Customer Data solely for the purpose of providing the Services.

13. Warranties and Disclaimers

13.1 The Provider warrants that the Services will materially conform to the documentation in Schedule 1 and will be provided with reasonable skill and care.

13.2 Except as expressly stated, the Services are provided "as is" and all implied warranties are excluded to the maximum extent permitted by law.

14. Indemnities

14.1 The Provider shall indemnify the Customer against third-party claims alleging that the Customer's authorised use of the Services infringes IPR, provided the Customer promptly notifies the Provider and allows the Provider sole control of the defence.

14.2 The Customer shall indemnify the Provider against claims arising from Customer Data or use of the Services in breach of this Agreement or Applicable Law.

15. Liability

15.1 Neither Party excludes liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, fraud, or any liability which cannot be excluded by law.

15.2 Subject to clause 15.1, each Party's aggregate liability in any twelve (12) month period shall not exceed the total Subscription Fees paid or payable by the Customer in that period.

15.3 Neither Party shall be liable for indirect or consequential loss, including loss of profit, revenue, goodwill or data.

16. Term and Termination

16.1 Initial Term: two (2) years from the Commencement Date. Thereafter, automatic one (1) year renewals unless either Party gives not less than thirty (30) days' notice prior to expiry.

16.2 Either Party may terminate for material breach not remedied within thirty (30) days of notice, or upon insolvency of the other Party.

16.3 On termination, access ceases. Exit assistance and data return are addressed in clause 17 and Schedule 3.

17. Exit and Data Return

17.1 During the thirty (30) days following termination or expiry, the Customer may export Customer Data via available tools or request a one-off export in a mutually agreed format.

17.2 Upon Customer request, the Provider shall delete or return Customer Data and certify deletion, subject to legal retention obligations.

18. Audit and Compliance

On reasonable notice and during business hours, the Customer may review relevant policies and third-party certifications. Any on-site audits shall be subject to reasonable security and confidentiality controls. The scope of such reviews may include, where relevant, environmental and sustainability certifications or reports relating to the provision of the Services.

19. Force Majeure

Neither Party is liable for delay or failure to perform due to events beyond its reasonable control, provided it notifies the other Party and mitigates. If a Force Majeure event continues for more than sixty (60) days, either Party may terminate on ten (10) Business Days' notice.

20. Sustainability

Environmental Commitment

Each Party shall, to the extent reasonable and proportionate, seek to reduce the greenhouse gas emissions associated with their activities under this Agreement, including through measures such as renewable energy procurement, efficient coding and infrastructure management, and reduced business travel.

21. General

21.1 Confidentiality. Each Party shall keep the other's Confidential Information confidential.

21.2 Assignment. The Customer may not assign without Provider consent; the Provider may assign as part of a corporate reorganisation or sale of business.

21.3 Notices. As set out in Schedule 1, Section 6.

21.4 Entire Agreement. This Agreement and Schedules constitute the entire agreement.

21.5 Governing Law. England and Wales; courts of England have exclusive jurisdiction.

Schedule 3 — Data Protection Addendum (DPA)

1. Roles

Customer: Controller; Provider: Processor.

2. Subject Matter and Duration

Processing of Customer personnel data for the provision of the Services during the Term and any exit period.

3. Nature and Purpose

User account management, authentication, audit logging, collaboration and support.

4. Categories of Data

  • Personal Data: name, email, role, activity logs, IP addresses, file metadata
  • Data Subjects: Customer employees/contractors; optionally Customer clients if invited as users

5. Processor Obligations

  1. Process only on documented instructions; maintain confidentiality; implement appropriate security.
  2. Sub-processors require Customer consent; Provider maintains an online list and will notify changes.
  3. No international transfers outside UK/EEA without adequate safeguards (e.g., UK IDTA/EU SCCs).
  4. Notify Personal Data Breach without undue delay (within 48 hours where feasible) and cooperate on remediation.
  5. Assist with data subject requests and DPIAs.
  6. Delete or return Personal Data on termination at Customer's option (subject to legal retention).
  7. Allow reasonable audits of compliance (subject to confidentiality and security).
