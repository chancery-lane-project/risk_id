1. Parties

Client: Alpha Property Group Ltd, a company incorporated in England and Wales (Company No. 09234567) whose registered office is at 123 Main Street, London, W1A 2BC, United Kingdom ("Client").

Service Provider: Omega Facilities Services Ltd, a company incorporated in England and Wales (Company No. 07890123) whose registered office is at 45 Industrial Estate Road, Birmingham, B2 4HF, United Kingdom ("Service Provider").

Client and Service Provider are each a "Party" and together the "Parties".

2. Background

  1. The Client owns and/or manages the Premises and requires the provision of integrated facilities management services.
  2. The Service Provider represents that it has the expertise, personnel, systems and experience necessary to deliver such services.
  3. The Parties have agreed the terms and conditions set out in this Agreement.

3. Definitions and Interpretation

3.1 Definitions

In this Agreement:

  • Applicable Law means all laws, statutes, regulations, by-laws, directives, codes of practice and guidance applicable to the Services, including health and safety and environmental laws.
  • Business Day means a day other than a Saturday, Sunday or public holiday in England.
  • Change has the meaning given in Schedule 8 (Change Control Procedure).
  • Commencement Date means 1 October 2025.
  • Confidential Information means all information disclosed by a Party that is marked confidential or would reasonably be considered confidential.
  • Data Protection Legislation means UK GDPR, the Data Protection Act 2018, and any successor or related legislation.
  • Force Majeure Event means an event beyond a Party's reasonable control as described in clause 22.
  • KPIs means the key performance indicators set out in Schedule 1.
  • Premises means the Client locations listed in Schedule 1, as updated from time to time by written notice.
  • Services means the facilities management services described in Schedule 1.
  • Service Credits means the credits payable for failure to meet Service Levels as set out in Schedule 3.
  • Service Levels means the service levels set out in Schedule 1.
  • TUPE means the Transfer of Undertakings (Protection of Employment) Regulations 2006 (as amended).

3.2 Interpretation

  • Headings are for convenience only and do not affect interpretation.
  • Words in the singular include the plural and vice versa.
  • References to "include" or "including" are deemed to be without limitation.

4. Term

This Agreement commences on the Commencement Date and continues for an initial term of three (3) years (the "Initial Term"). It shall automatically renew for successive periods of one (1) year (each a "Renewal Term") unless either Party gives not less than ninety (90) days' written notice prior to the end of the Initial Term or the then-current Renewal Term.

5. Services and Standards

5.1 The Service Provider shall provide the Services in a diligent, professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with Good Industry Practice, the Service Levels and Applicable Law.

5.2 The Services include, without limitation:

  • cleaning and janitorial
  • manned guarding and electronic security
  • mechanical, electrical and plumbing (MEP) maintenance
  • helpdesk and reactive works
  • grounds maintenance and landscaping
  • waste management
  • statutory inspections and compliance (e.g., fire safety, lifts, water hygiene)
  • soft services as specified in Schedule 1

5.3 The Service Provider shall maintain and operate a 24/7 helpdesk for logging, prioritising, dispatching and closing service requests, with unique job IDs and audit trails.

5.4 All personnel shall be suitably qualified, trained, vetted (including right to work checks), properly supervised and provided with appropriate personal protective equipment.

5.5 The Service Provider shall implement planned preventive maintenance (PPM) regimes aligned to manufacturer recommendations, SFG20 or equivalent standards, and maintain asset registers and PPM schedules updated quarterly.

6. Service Management, Reporting and Meetings

6.1 Service Reviews. Monthly operational reviews and quarterly strategic reviews shall be held; agendas shall include performance against KPIs, incidents, health & safety, Change requests, and continuous improvement.

6.2 Reports. The Service Provider shall provide monthly performance reports within five (5) Business Days of month-end including KPI dashboard, Service Credits (if any), incident log, PPM completion rates, statutory compliance status, energy/waste metrics and open risks/issues.

6.3 Audit Rights. The Client may audit the Service Provider's records, systems and Premises on reasonable notice to verify compliance and charges. The Service Provider shall cooperate and promptly implement agreed remediation actions.

7. Change Control

Changes to the scope, Service Levels, Premises or charges shall be managed in accordance with Schedule 8 (Change Control Procedure). No Change is binding unless agreed in writing by both Parties.

8. Charges, Indexation and Payment

8.1 Charges. The Client shall pay the fixed annual charge set out in Schedule 2 (the "Charges") in four equal quarterly instalments in advance, against valid VAT invoices.

8.2 Variable Works. Reactive works and additional services requested by the Client outside the fixed scope shall be charged at the rates in Schedule 2 or as otherwise agreed via Change Control.

8.3 Indexation. With effect from each anniversary of the Commencement Date, the fixed element of the Charges shall be adjusted by the percentage increase (if any) in the Consumer Prices Index (CPI) for the preceding 12-month period, capped at 5% per annum unless otherwise agreed.

8.4 Payment Terms. Invoices are payable within thirty (30) days of the invoice date. Late payments shall accrue interest pursuant to the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998.

8.5 VAT. All Charges are exclusive of VAT, which shall be payable in addition where applicable.

9. Compliance, Policies and Health & Safety

9.1 The Service Provider shall at all times comply with Applicable Law and the policies listed in Schedule 5 (Policies and Standards), as provided to the Service Provider and updated on reasonable notice.

9.2 The Service Provider shall maintain robust health and safety systems, including risk assessments, method statements, permits to work and incident reporting in accordance with RIDDOR. Any notifiable incidents shall be reported to the Client within twenty-four (24) hours.

9.3 Environmental compliance shall include lawful waste handling, segregation and disposal, and records of waste transfer/consignment notes retained for at least three (3) years.

10. Key Personnel and Staffing

10.1 The Service Provider shall assign the Key Personnel identified in Schedule 4. Replacement of Key Personnel requires the Client's prior written consent (not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed).

10.2 The Service Provider is responsible for all employment obligations relating to its personnel and shall ensure compliance with right-to-work, wage and working time requirements.

10.3 If TUPE applies on commencement or expiry/termination, the Parties shall cooperate in good faith and comply with their respective TUPE obligations.

11. Intellectual Property

11.1 All pre-existing IPR of a Party remains vested in that Party.

11.2 All reports, operating procedures and documentation specifically created for the Client under this Agreement ("Deliverables") shall belong to the Client upon payment in full. The Service Provider retains a non-exclusive licence to use Deliverables solely for the purpose of performing the Services.

12. Data Protection

Where the Service Provider processes Personal Data on behalf of the Client, the Data Protection Addendum at Schedule 6 applies and forms part of this Agreement.

13. Confidentiality

13.1 Each Party shall keep the other Party's Confidential Information strictly confidential and shall not disclose it except to personnel who need to know it for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by obligations of confidentiality.

13.2 Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that is public, independently developed, or required to be disclosed by law or a competent authority.

14. Insurance

The Service Provider shall maintain, with reputable insurers, the insurances specified in Schedule 7 with the minimum limits therein. Upon request, the Service Provider shall provide certificates of insurance and evidence of premium payment.

15. Liability and Indemnities

15.1 The Service Provider shall indemnify the Client against losses arising from:

  • death or personal injury caused by the Service Provider's negligence
  • damage to tangible property caused by the Service Provider
  • the Service Provider's breach of Applicable Law, including health and safety and environmental law

15.2 Subject to clause 15.3, each Party's aggregate liability in any Contract Year shall not exceed one hundred percent (100%) of the total Charges paid or payable in that Contract Year.

15.3 Nothing in this Agreement limits or excludes liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation, or any other liability which cannot lawfully be limited or excluded.

15.4 Neither Party shall be liable for any loss of profit, loss of revenue, loss of business or any indirect or consequential loss.

16. Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery

The Service Provider shall maintain and annually test business continuity and disaster recovery plans appropriate to the Services and provide summaries to the Client upon request. Invocation of such plans shall be promptly notified to the Client.

17. Subcontracting and Assignment

17.1 The Service Provider shall not assign or transfer this Agreement without the Client's prior written consent (not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed). The Service Provider may subcontract elements of the Services but remains liable for subcontractor performance.

17.2 The Client may assign its rights and obligations to any group company or successor in connection with a bona fide business reorganisation.

18. Service Failures and Service Credits

Failure to meet Service Levels shall entitle the Client to Service Credits as calculated in Schedule 3. Payment or deduction of Service Credits is without prejudice to other remedies.

19. Benchmarking and Continuous Improvement

On the first anniversary of the Commencement Date and annually thereafter, the Parties shall review the Services for opportunities to improve efficiency, sustainability and value for money. Any resulting changes shall be agreed through the Change Control Procedure.

20. Records and Retention

The Service Provider shall maintain complete and accurate records relating to the Services, including job logs, compliance certificates, PPM records and invoicing support, for at least six (6) years following the end of the Agreement.

21. Force Majeure

Neither Party shall be in breach or liable for delay or failure to perform due to a Force Majeure Event, provided that the affected Party promptly notifies the other Party and uses reasonable endeavours to mitigate the effects. If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) days, either Party may terminate this Agreement on ten (10) Business Days' written notice.

22. Suspension and Termination

22.1 Convenience. Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience on not less than ninety (90) days' written notice, to expire no earlier than the end of the Initial Term.

22.2 Cause. Either Party may terminate with immediate effect by written notice if the other Party commits a material breach which (if remediable) is not remedied within thirty (30) days of notice, or becomes insolvent.

22.3 Persistent Breach. Repeated failure to meet Service Levels in any three (3) consecutive months shall be deemed a material breach.

22.4 Suspension. The Client may suspend specific Services where necessary to protect health, safety, security or compliance, provided that Charges are equitably adjusted.

23. Exit Management and Handover

23.1 Within thirty (30) days of the Commencement Date the Service Provider shall develop an exit plan consistent with Schedule 9 and keep it updated.

23.2 Upon expiry or termination, the Service Provider shall provide reasonable assistance to transition the Services to the Client or a replacement provider, including transfer of accurate asset lists, maintenance records, warranties and access credentials, and offering employment information to the extent permitted by law.

24. Dispute Resolution

24.1 Disputes shall first be escalated to the Parties' contract managers and then to senior executives. If unresolved within twenty (20) Business Days, either Party may refer the dispute to mediation under the CEDR Model Mediation Procedure.

24.2 Nothing prevents either Party seeking injunctive or other interim relief at any time.

25. Notices

Notices shall be in writing and delivered by hand, pre-paid first-class post or email to the addresses set out below (or as otherwise notified). Notices are deemed received: if delivered by hand, on delivery; by post, two (2) Business Days after posting; by email, when sent provided no bounce-back is received.

26. General

26.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement (including the Schedules) constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes prior discussions.

26.2 Variation. No variation is effective unless in writing and signed by authorised representatives of both Parties.

26.3 Severance. If any provision is held invalid, it shall be deemed modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid.

26.4 No Partnership. Nothing creates a partnership, joint venture or agency relationship between the Parties.

26.5 Third Party Rights. A person who is not a Party has no rights under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999 to enforce any term of this Agreement.

26.6 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts.

26.7 Governing Law and Jurisdiction. This Agreement is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales and the Parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.
